CHAPTER 16: BLUE MINOR

Style: Minor Blues | Key: C Minor | Tempo: 110 BPM | Time: 4/4

OVERVIEW

"The essential minor blues form with melodic minor colors."

Blue Minor fills a critical gap in the Trio Tunes collection. While Blue Cycle and Greezy cover major blues, the minor blues is an equally essential form that every jazz guitarist must master. The key of C minor provides clarity for learning the minor ii-V-I vocabulary.

Playing Tip: Let the minor 3rd (Eb) ring—it defines the minor blues sound.

THE FORM

12-bar minor blues with melodic minor voicings:

| Cm(maj7) | Cm6      | Fm9      | Fm6      |
| Cm(maj7) | Cm7      | Abmaj7   | G7alt    |
| Dm7b5    | G7alt    | Cm(maj7) | Dm7b5 G7 |

Key Harmonic Features

  1. Melodic minor tonic: Cm(maj7) — the sophisticated minor blues sound
  2. Minor ii-V: Dm7b5 → G7alt — the essential minor cadence
  3. bVImaj7: Abmaj7 — borrowed from natural minor, classic minor blues color
  4. Cm6: Implies C Dorian (natural 6th)

SCALE PALETTE

Chord Primary Scale Minor Blues Color
Cm(maj7) C Melodic Minor Maj7 + min3 = sophisticated tension
Cm6 C Dorian Natural 6th (A natural)
Fm9 F Dorian IV chord in minor

Melodic Minor Mastery

The minor blues is a gateway to melodic minor vocabulary:

  • C melodic minor over Cm(maj7): C-D-Eb-F-G-A-B
  • F Dorian over Fm7: F-G-Ab-Bb-C-D-Eb
  • Ab melodic minor over G7alt: Ab-Bb-Cb-Db-Eb-F-G

TRIAD PAIR VOCABULARY

THE THREE CHORUSES

Chorus 1: Lyrical Minor (Version A)

Concept: Singing melodic lines in minor

  • Emphasize the minor 3rd (Eb) and major 7th (B) tension
  • Use the melodic minor ascending, natural minor descending
  • Long tones on chord tones, chromatic approaches

Key interval: The major 7th to minor 3rd (B to Eb) — the defining Cm(maj7) sound.

Chorus 2: Altered Dominants (Version B)

Concept: Focus on the V7alt resolution

  • Over G7alt: Use all altered tones (b9, #9, b5, #5)
  • Ab melodic minor gives you b9 (Ab), #9 (Bb), b5 (Db), #5 (Eb)
  • Resolve chromatic approaches into Cm

Practice: Isolate bars 9-10 (Dm7b5 → G7alt) and master the minor ii-V.

Chorus 3: Wyble-Style Counterpoint (Version C)

Concept: Two-voice minor blues

  • Bass: Root motion (C-F-C-Ab-G-C)
  • Melody: Independent line on strings 1-2
  • Create voice independence in the minor context

VOICING REFERENCE

Minor Tonic Voicings

Cm(maj7): x-3-5-4-4-x (root position, melodic minor sound)
Cm6:      x-3-5-2-4-x (Dorian color)
Cm9:      x-3-1-3-3-x (extended minor)

Minor ii-V Voicings

Dm7b5:    x-5-6-5-6-x (half-diminished)
G7alt:    3-x-3-4-4-x (altered dominant)
G7#9:     3-x-3-4-4-6 (Hendrix-style altered)

Borrowed Chord Voicings

Abmaj7:   4-x-5-5-4-x (bVI chord)
Fm9:      x-8-8-8-8-x (IV minor)

COMMON PITFALLS

  1. Playing natural minor only: Melodic minor is the sophisticated choice
  2. Ignoring the altered dominant: G7alt is where the tension lives
  3. Rushing the turnaround: Bars 11-12 need space and clarity
  4. Forgetting the bVI: Abmaj7 is a key color — don't skip it
