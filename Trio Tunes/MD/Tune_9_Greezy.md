## 2. SCALE PALETTE STUDY

*   **Primary Universe (G7):** G Mixolydian / G Blues Scale (G-Bb-C-Db-D-F).
*   **Subdominant (C9):** C Mixolydian.
*   **Diminished Connectors (G#°7, C#°7):** Octatonic (Whole-Half Diminished).
*   **Altered Dominants (E7#9, D7#9):** Blues Scale / Altered Scale.

**Blues Shuffle Logic:**
*   **Triplet subdivision** — every beat divides into three.
*   **Swing the 8ths** — long-short feel, not straight.
*   **Mix major and minor 3rds** freely (the "blue" note).
*   **Diminished chords** act as chromatic connectors between I and IV.

**Barry Harris Application:**
*   On **G7 → G#°7**, use **G Major 6 Diminished** scale.
*   On **C9 → C#°7**, use **C Major 6 Diminished** scale.

## 4. THREE ETUDE CHORUSES

### **Chorus 1: Classic Shuffle Riff (Groove)**
*   **Concept:** Repetitive, hypnotic 12/8 riff; think B.B. King meets Grant Green.
*   **Motif:** G-B-D triplet pattern with b7 (F) grace note.
*   **Bars 1-4:** Establish main riff on G7; slight variation bar 4.
*   **Bars 5-6:** Transpose riff to C9; chromatic slide bar 6.
*   **Bars 9-12:** Turnaround with walking bass line in left hand (if chord-melody).

### **Chorus 2: Diminished Connector Study**
*   **Concept:** Emphasize the °7 connectors with diminished arpeggios.
*   **Bar 4 (G#°7):** G#-B-D-F arpeggio (ascending).
*   **Bar 6 (C#°7):** C#-E-G-Bb arpeggio (descending).
*   **Goal:** Hear how diminished chords "lean" into the next chord.

### **Chorus 3: Blue Note Chromaticism**
*   **Concept:** Exploit the b3/3 ambiguity throughout.
*   **Texture:**
    *   *Bar 1-2:* Bb (b3) sliding into B (3) repeatedly.
    *   *Bar 5:* Eb (b3 of C) sliding into E.
    *   *Bar 8 (E7#9):* G natural (b3/#9) is the target note.
*   **Key Moment (Bar 10 — D7#9):** F natural (b3) over D7 creates maximum grease.
