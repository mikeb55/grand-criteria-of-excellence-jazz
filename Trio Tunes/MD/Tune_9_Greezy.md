## 2. SCALE PALETTE STUDY

*   **Primary Universe (G7):** G Mixolydian / G Blues Scale.
*   **Secondary Universe (C7):** C Mixolydian / C Blues Scale.
*   **Diminished Connectors (G#dim7, C#dim7):** Half-Whole Diminished scales.
*   **Turnaround (E7):** E Mixolydian / E Blues Scale.
*   **Altered Dominant (D7#9):** D Half-Whole Diminished for the "crunch."

**Barry Harris Logic:**
*   **G#dim7 (Bar 4)** connects G7 to Am7 (or C7) chromatically.
*   **C#dim7 (Bar 6)** connects C7 to D7 chromatically.
*   Treat diminished chords as **passing dominants** (G#dim7 = E7b9, C#dim7 = A7b9).

## 4. THREE ETUDE CHORUSES

### **Chorus 1: Classic Blues (BB King Style)**
*   **Concept:** Traditional blues vocabulary with triplet phrasing.
*   **Motif:** Call and response — 2-bar phrases.
*   **Bars 1-4:** Statement with bends on b3 and b7.
*   **Bars 5-8:** Answer over IV chord with resolution to I.
*   **Bars 9-12:** Turnaround with classic ii-V licks.
*   **Feel:** Behind the beat; vocal, expressive bends.

### **Chorus 2: Diminished Connections**
*   **Concept:** Use diminished arpeggios as chromatic connectors.
*   **Bar 4 (G#dim7):** Arpeggio G#-B-D-F descending, resolving to G.
*   **Bar 6 (C#dim7):** Arpeggio C#-E-G-Bb ascending, resolving to D.
*   **Bar 10 (D7#9):** Half-whole diminished patterns.
*   **Goal:** Modern blues sound with chromatic sophistication.

### **Chorus 3: Two-Voice Blues**
*   **Concept:** Bass line against melody, solo blues style.
*   **Texture:**
    *   *Top Voice:* Blues licks on strings 1-3.
    *   *Bottom Voice:* Root notes on beats 1 and 3 (in 12/8 grouping).
*   **Key Moment (Bar 6 - C#dim7):** Bass walks chromatically C-C#-D while melody holds.
*   **Ending:** Convergence on G7 chord with "turnaround tag."
