## 2. SCALE PALETTE STUDY

*   **Primary Hexatonic (BMaj7):** B-C#-D#-F#-G#-A# (Major Hexatonic — omit the 4th).
*   **Minor Hexatonic (G#m7, C#m7, D#m7):** Minor versions of the same 6-note concept.
*   **Lydian Hexatonic (Emaj7#11):** E-F#-G#-A#-B-D# (Lydian with #11).
*   **Suspended Resolution (F#7sus4):** F#-B-C#-E (sus4 resolves within the bar).

**Hexatonic Logic:**
*   All melodies use only 6 notes from the parent scale — creates the "hexagonal" sound.
*   The missing 4th degree (E in B major) creates openness.
*   5/4 groove divides as 3+2 (first three beats, then two).

## 4. THREE ETUDE CHORUSES

### **Chorus A: Lyrical Melody (5/4 Phrasing)**
*   **Concept:** Long phrases that span the 3+2 grouping naturally.
*   **Motif:** Ascending hexatonic (B-C#-D#) on beats 1-2-3, held note on 4-5.
*   **Bars 1-4:** Establish the 5/4 feel with half-note + dotted-half phrasing.
*   **Bars 5-8:** Invert the motif — descending on 1-2-3, ascending on 4-5.
*   **Bars 9-12:** Bridge builds intensity; final bar cadences on beat 5.

### **Chorus B: Modern Triad-Pair Lines**
*   **Concept:** Continuous 8th notes in groups of 3+2 within the 5/4 bar.
*   **Bar 1-2 (BMaj7):** B/C# pair — 3 notes ascending, 2 notes descending, repeat.
*   **Bar 5-6 (BMaj7-C#m7):** Transition from B/C# to C#/D# pair.
*   **Bar 9-10 (Emaj7#11-D#m7):** E/F# Lydian pair melts into D#m.
*   **Goal:** Create flowing 8ths that respect the 3+2 internal pulse.

### **Chorus C: Wyble Two-Voice Counterpoint**
*   **Concept:** Bass line emphasizes beats 1 and 4 (the downbeats of 3+2).
*   **Texture:**
    *   *Top Voice:* Hexatonic melody using half notes and quarters.
    *   *Bottom Voice:* Quarter notes on 1 and 4, silence on 2-3 and 5.
*   **Key Moment (Bar 4 - E/B):** Bass holds B pedal; melody walks E-F#-G#-A#-B.
*   **Key Moment (Bar 9 - Emaj7#11):** Bass plays E on 1, A# on 4; melody holds.
*   **Ending:** Both voices converge on B octave.

### **Chorus D: Hybrid Synthesis**
*   **Concept:** Combines lyrical phrasing, triad-pair bursts, and 3+2 counterpoint.
*   **Bars 1-4:** Lyrical melody (Chorus A) with bass on 1 and 4.
*   **Bars 5-8:** Triad-pair runs (Chorus B) replace held notes.
*   **Bars 9-12:** Full counterpoint (Chorus C) with dynamic build.
*   **Dynamic Arc:** mp (1-4) → mf (5-8) → f (9-11) → p (12).

## 6. 5/4 RHYTHM GUIDE

*   **Internal Grouping:** 3+2 (count "ONE-two-three, FOUR-five").
*   **Accent Pattern:** Strong on 1, secondary on 4.
*   **Phrasing:** Let phrases start on 1 or 4; avoid starting on 2, 3, or 5.
*   **Walking Bass:** Quarter notes can emphasize 1-4-1-4 pattern.
