## 4. THREE ETUDE CHORUSES

### **Chorus A: Lyrical Melody (7/8 Groove)**
*   **Concept:** Rhythmic melody that locks with the 2+2+3 subdivision.
*   **Motif:** Quarter-quarter-dotted-quarter rhythm (2+2+3).
*   **Bars 1-4:** Establish the 7/8 feel with strong downbeat melody.
*   **Bars 5-8:** Melody moves through relative major colors.
*   **Bars 9-12:** ii-V tension; melody emphasizes altered extensions.
*   **Bars 13-16:** Return to vamp; turnaround to repeat.

### **Chorus B: Modern Triad-Pair Lines**
*   **Concept:** Rapid 8th notes in groups of 2+2+3.
*   **Bar 1-2 (C#m7):** C# Minor triad (2 beats) + E Major triad (2 beats) + G# Minor (3 beats).
*   **Bar 9-10 (D#m7b5-G#7alt):** Half-dim arpeggio into altered triads.
*   **Goal:** Continuous 8ths that respect the 7/8 accent pattern.

### **Chorus C: Wyble Two-Voice Counterpoint**
*   **Concept:** Ostinato bass riff against syncopated melody.
*   **Texture:**
    *   *Bottom Voice:* Repeating 7/8 bass riff (C#-E-C#-G#-F#-E-D#).
    *   *Top Voice:* Syncopated melody that plays "against" the bass.
*   **Key Moment (Bar 9-10):** Bass riff shifts chromatically; melody sustains.
*   **Ending:** Both voices land on unison C#.

### **Chorus D: Hybrid Synthesis**
*   **Concept:** Combines groove melody, triad-pair bursts, and bass ostinato.
*   **Bars 1-4:** Groove melody (Chorus A) over bass ostinato.
*   **Bars 5-8:** Triad-pair runs (Chorus B) replace sustained notes.
*   **Bars 9-12:** Full counterpoint (Chorus C) builds intensity.
*   **Bars 13-16:** Return to groove; climactic ending.
*   **Dynamic Arc:** f → ff → fff → f.
