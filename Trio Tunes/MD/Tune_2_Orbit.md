## 2. SCALE PALETTE STUDY

*   **Primary Universe (FMaj7#11):** F Lydian — emphasize the #4 (B natural) for open color.
*   **Chromatic Pools (DbMaj7, Ebm7):** Db Ionian / Eb Dorian — creates the "Shorter cloud" effect.
*   **Distant Tension (Bm7b5 → E7alt):** B Locrian → E Altered Scale — unresolved tritone pair.
*   **Neapolitan Approach (GbMaj7):** Gb Lydian — floats above the final cadence.

**Wayne Shorter Logic:**
*   Avoid resolving the E7alt to A; instead, let AMaj7 function as color, not resolution.
*   The CmMaj7 (Bar 11) should be voiced with the major 7th on top for maximum tension.
*   GbMaj7 (Bar 15) approaches the tonic by half-step — classic Shorter voice-leading.

## 4. THREE ETUDE CHORUSES

### **Chorus A: Lyrical Melody (Motivic)**
*   **Concept:** Long, singing phrases that arc across bar lines in the waltz feel.
*   **Motif:** Rising major 7th interval (F to E) as the signature gesture.
*   **Bars 1-4:** Establish floating theme with sustained dotted-half notes on strong beats.
*   **Bars 5-8:** Motif inversion — descending major 7th (E to F an octave below).
*   **Bars 9-12:** Rhythmic augmentation — half notes become dotted halves with pick-up gestures.
*   **Bars 13-16:** Return of opening motif with added upper neighbor tones.

### **Chorus B: Modern Triad-Pair Lines**
*   **Concept:** Continuous 8th-note triplets using the Triad Pairs from Section 3.
*   **Bar 1-2 (FMaj7#11):** F Triad ascending, G Triad descending — Lydian cascade.
*   **Bar 5-6 (FMaj7#11 → Bm7b5):** Voice-lead F triad into B diminished by common tone (B).
*   **Bar 7-8 (E7alt → AMaj7):** Bb Major arpeggio smashing into A Major — jarring yet connected.
*   **Bar 15-16 (GbMaj7 → FMaj7):** Gb triad melts chromatically into F triad.
*   **Goal:** Angular, unpredictable line that still breathes with the 3/4 pulse.

### **Chorus C: Wyble Two-Voice Counterpoint**
*   **Concept:** Independent bass line against a sustained melody in waltz rhythm.
*   **Texture:**
    *   *Top Voice:* Dotted-half note melody sustains (Strings 1-2).
    *   *Bottom Voice:* Walking quarter-note bass (Strings 5-6), often contrary motion.
*   **Key Moment (Bar 11 - CmMaj7):** Top voice holds high Eb; Bottom voice walks C-D-Eb-E (chromatic ascent).
*   **Key Moment (Bar 15 - GbMaj7):** Top voice Bb; Bottom voice Gb-Ab-A-Bb (meets melody).
*   **Ending:** Both voices converge on unison F, then expand to F-D (6th interval) on final chord.

### **Chorus D: Hybrid Synthesis**
*   **Concept:** Combines lyrical phrasing with contrapuntal texture and triad-pair bursts.
*   **Bars 1-4:** Lyrical melody (Chorus A material) with sparse bass notes.
*   **Bars 5-8:** Triad-pair runs (Chorus B) interrupt the lyrical flow.
*   **Bars 9-12:** Full two-voice counterpoint (Chorus C) creates textural climax.
*   **Bars 13-16:** Return to lyrical theme, bass walks to final unison, expands to 6th.
*   **Dynamic Arc:** p (bars 1-4) → mf (5-8) → f (9-12) → mp (13-16 with ritardando).
