## 2. SCALE PALETTE STUDY

*   **Primary Universe (Dmaj9):** D Lydian for brightness; D Ionian for stability.
*   **Secondary Universe (Em9):** E Dorian with natural 6th.
*   **Modal Interchange (Ebmaj7, Abmaj7):** Eb Lydian, Ab Lydian — chromatic approaches.
*   **Melodic Minor Application:** D melodic minor over Dm7 (bar 13).
*   **Altered Dominant (A7alt):** A Super Locrian for maximum tension before resolution.

**Jobim Logic:**
*   Use **D major pedal** in bass throughout A sections.
*   On **Ebmaj7 (bar 9)**, the chromatic root motion (D → Eb) creates the characteristic bossa color.
*   The **Cmaj7 (bar 15)** functions as a "neighbor" chord before returning to D.

## 4. THREE ETUDE CHORUSES

### **Chorus 1: Lyrical Melody (Jobim-Inspired)**
*   **Concept:** Singing, arch-shaped phrases over the bossa rhythm.
*   **Bars 1-4:** Ascending phrase targeting F# (3rd of D).
*   **Bars 5-8:** Descending answer resolving to D.
*   **Bridge (9-16):** Chromatic exploration through distant keys.
*   **Phrasing:** Long notes on downbeats; syncopated answers.

### **Chorus 2: Melodic Minor Colors**
*   **Concept:** Modern bossa using melodic minor modes.
*   **Bar 1-8 (A Section):** D melodic minor over D pedal creates subtle F natural tension.
*   **Bar 13 (Dm7):** D melodic minor ascending.
*   **Bar 16 (A7alt):** A altered scale with emphasis on Bb and Eb (b9 and #11).
*   **Goal:** Add modern harmonic color while maintaining bossa feel.

### **Chorus 3: Melody + Bass Counterpoint**
*   **Concept:** Simultaneous melody and walking bass.
*   **Texture:**
    *   *Top Voice:* Lyrical melody (strings 1-2).
    *   *Bottom Voice:* Root motion on beats 1 and 3 (strings 4-6).
*   **Key Technique:** Thumb independence for bass; fingers for melody.
*   **Challenge Level:** High — start at 60 BPM.
