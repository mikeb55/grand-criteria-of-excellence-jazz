# 1. INTERVAL DNA

## 1.1 Core Interval Signatures

| ID | Interval | Semitones | Character | Primary Influence | Usage Context |
|:--:|:---------|:---------:|:----------|:------------------|:--------------|
| I-1 | **Perfect 4th (P4)** | 5 | Open, modal, suspended | McCoy Tyner, Metheny | Quartal melodies, open voicings |
| I-2 | **Major 7th (M7)** | 11 | Luminous, wide, yearning | Shorter, Rosenwinkel | Melodic leaps, climax points |
| I-3 | **Minor 6th (m6)** | 8 | Melancholic, expressive | Jim Hall, Frisell | Ballads, descending lines |
| I-4 | **#11 (Tritone)** | 6 | Lydian, floating, bright | Metheny, Maria Schneider | Lydian passages, tension |
| I-5 | **2–6–♭3–#4 Cell** | — | Modern angular | Rosenwinkel, Monder | Angular phrases, modern lines |

## 1.2 Interval Usage Rules

- **Every 4-bar phrase** must contain at least one core interval signature.
- **Climax points** favor M7 or m6 leaps.
- **Angular sections** emphasize the 2–6–♭3–#4 cell.
- **Modal passages** prioritize P4 stacks.
- **Avoid:** Excessive stepwise motion (more than 4 consecutive scale tones).

## 1.3 Interval DNA by Style Context

| Context | Primary Intervals | Secondary | Avoid |
|:--------|:------------------|:----------|:------|
| Lyrical/Ballad | m6, M7, P5 | M2, m3 | Tritone stacks |
| Bebop | m3, M3, P5, m7 | M2, P4 | Wide compound intervals |
| Angular/Modern | P4, #11, m6 | m7, M2 | Simple triadic motion |
| Blues | m3/M3 crossover, b7 | P4, P5 | Lydian intervals |
| Fusion | P4 stacks, M7 | #11, m9 | Conventional voice leading |

# 2. RHYTHMIC DNA

## 2.1 Core Rhythmic Cells

| ID | Pattern | Subdivision | Character | Primary Influence |
|:--:|:--------|:------------|:----------|:------------------|
| R-1 | **Swing 8ths** | Triplet-based | Classic jazz flow | Parker, Stitt, Benson |
| R-2 | **Straight 16ths** | Even subdivision | Modern, fusion | Scofield, Rosenwinkel |
| R-3 | **Dotted Quarter Displacement** | Across barline | Floating, metric ambiguity | Shorter, Metheny |
| R-4 | **3+3+2 Grouping** | Additive | Latin-inflected, driving | Scofield, modern jazz |
| R-5 | **Polyrhythmic 3:2** | Cross-rhythm | Tension, complexity | Monder, Halvorson |
| R-6 | **Space & Breath** | Rests as music | Sparse, deliberate | Hall, Frisell, Monk |
| R-7 | **Metric Modulation** | Tempo illusion | Surprise, sophistication | Metheny, Ant Law |

## 2.2 Rhythmic DNA Rules

- **Every 8-bar section** must employ at least two rhythmic cells.
- **Climax sections** increase rhythmic density (R-2, R-4).
- **Ballads and intros** emphasize R-6 (Space & Breath).
- **Turnarounds** often use R-3 (Displacement) for forward motion.
- **Blues passages** favor R-1 with occasional R-4.

## 2.3 Rhythmic Application by Style

| Style | Primary Rhythm | Secondary | Avoid |
|:------|:---------------|:----------|:------|
| Traditional Blues | R-1 (Swing) | R-6 (Space) | R-5 (Polyrhythm) |
| Bebop | R-1 (Swing), 8th-note runs | R-3 (Displacement) | Excessive R-6 |
| Modern/Angular | R-2, R-4, R-5 | R-3, R-7 | Predictable downbeats |
| Ballad | R-6 (Space), rubato | R-3 | Dense 16th patterns |
| Fusion | R-2, R-4, R-7 | R-5 | Traditional swing |

# 3. TRIAD-PAIR DNA

## 3.1 Core Triad-Pair Relationships

| ID | Pair Type | Interval | Sound | Primary Use |
|:--:|:----------|:---------|:------|:------------|
| TP-1 | **Major + Major (whole step apart)** | M2 | Lydian, bright | Static harmony, pedal points |
| TP-2 | **Major + Minor (minor 3rd apart)** | m3 | Dorian, warm | Minor chords, modal vamps |
| TP-3 | **Major + Augmented** | Various | Altered, tension | Dominant alterations |
| TP-4 | **Minor + Minor (whole step apart)** | M2 | Dark, modal | Phrygian/Locrian contexts |
| TP-5 | **Tritone Pairs** | TT | Maximum tension | Alt dominants, outside playing |

## 3.2 Triad-Pair Application Matrix

| Chord Quality | Stable Triad | Tension Triad | Resulting Sound |
|:--------------|:-------------|:--------------|:----------------|
| Maj7 | Root Major | II Major | Lydian (1-3-5-7-9-#11-13) |
| Dom7 | Root Major | bVII Major | Mixolydian (1-3-5-b7-9-11-13) |
| Dom7alt | bII Major | Root Aug | Altered (1-b9-#9-3-b5-#5-b7) |
| Min7 | bIII Major | IV Major | Dorian (1-b3-4-5-6-b7-9) |
| Min7b5 | bIII Major | bVI Major | Locrian #2 |
| Dim7 | Any dim + HW scale | — | Diminished symmetric |

## 3.3 Triad-Pair Voicing Strategy

- **Ascending motion:** Start with stable triad, ascend into tension triad.
- **Descending motion:** Reverse for resolution effect.
- **Angular lines:** Alternate triads rapidly (every 2–3 notes).
- **Lyrical lines:** Sustain one triad longer before transitioning.

# 4. BLUES & BEBOP DNA

## 4.1 Blues DNA Components

| ID | Element | Description | Application |
|:--:|:--------|:------------|:------------|
| BL-1 | **Blue Note Inflection** | b3, b5, b7 bends and grace notes | All blues-influenced passages |
| BL-2 | **Call & Response** | 2-bar phrases answering each other | Blues form, Gospel influences |
| BL-3 | **Double-Stop 3rds/6ths** | Parallel harmony motion | Grant Green, Benson |
| BL-4 | **Minor Pentatonic Foundation** | 1-b3-4-5-b7 | Ground all blues phrases |
| BL-5 | **Blues Chromatic Enclosure** | Chromatic approach to chord tones | Bebop-blues hybrid |
| BL-6 | **Shuffle Feel (12/8)** | Triplet-based groove | Slow blues, R&B influenced |

## 4.2 Bebop DNA Components

| ID | Element | Description | Primary Influence |
|:--:|:--------|:------------|:------------------|
| BB-1 | **Bebop Scale Usage** | Added passing tones for beat alignment | Parker, Stitt |
| BB-2 | **Enclosure Patterns** | Chromatic + diatonic surrounding | Parker, Barry Harris |
| BB-3 | **Chord-Tone Targeting** | Land on 3rd, 7th on strong beats | All bebop masters |
| BB-4 | **1-2-3-5 Cell** | Universal bebop building block | Coltrane, Parker |
| BB-5 | **Diminished Approaches** | Dim7 arpeggios resolving down half-step | Barry Harris |
| BB-6 | **Double-Time Passages** | 16ths over swing feel | Parker, Stitt |
| BB-7 | **Guide Tone Lines** | 3rd to 7th voice leading | Functional ii-V-I |

## 4.3 Blues/Bebop Application Rules

- **Roots-influenced tunes:** Minimum 60% Blues DNA (BL-1 through BL-6).
- **Functional ii-V-I passages:** Mandatory Bebop DNA (BB-1 through BB-7).
- **Blues form tunes:** Blues DNA governs melodic shape; Bebop DNA adds sophistication.
- **Modern tunes:** Blues DNA as color; Bebop DNA as structural foundation.

# 5. EXPRESSIVE DNA (Phrasing & Contour)

## 5.1 Master Influence Profiles

### Jim Hall — Architectural Lyricism
- **Signature:** Space as compositional element
- **Phrasing:** Questions left unanswered, incomplete resolutions
- **Dynamics:** Extreme soft to medium; rarely loud
- **Contour:** Gently arching, breathing phrases
- **Application:** Ballads, duo settings, introspective moments

### Bill Frisell — Textural Ambiguity
- **Signature:** Blurred boundaries between melody and harmony
- **Phrasing:** Floating, ambient, sustained
- **Dynamics:** Volume swells, gradual builds
- **Contour:** Horizontal expansion, wide register use
- **Application:** Atmospheric sections, transitions, endings

### Mary Halvorson — Angular Disruption
- **Signature:** Unexpected pitch bends, microtonal inflections
- **Phrasing:** Deliberately awkward, off-center
- **Dynamics:** Sudden dynamic shifts
- **Contour:** Jagged, unpredictable leaps
- **Application:** Experimental sections, tension builders

### Pat Metheny — Melodic Optimism
- **Signature:** Wide intervals resolving to singing phrases
- **Phrasing:** Long, flowing, connected
- **Dynamics:** Building arcs, peak-and-valley
- **Contour:** Ascending trajectories, triumphant climaxes
- **Application:** Theme statements, endings, uplifting passages

### John Scofield — Gritty Articulation
- **Signature:** Chromatic slides, hammer-ons, bent notes
- **Phrasing:** Conversational, speech-like
- **Dynamics:** Punchy accents, rhythmic emphasis
- **Contour:** Circling, orbiting target notes
- **Application:** Funk/blues sections, groove-based passages

### Kurt Rosenwinkel — Vocal Linearity
- **Signature:** Multi-octave legato lines, voice-like phrases
- **Phrasing:** Continuous flow, minimal breath points
- **Dynamics:** Even, flowing, mezzo dynamic
- **Contour:** Vocal-range shapes, singable melodies
- **Application:** Modern ballads, flowing solos

### Ben Monder — Harmonic Density
- **Signature:** Cluster voicings, extended sustains
- **Phrasing:** Phrases emerging from texture
- **Dynamics:** Layered, orchestral builds
- **Contour:** Vertical expansion, chordal melodies
- **Application:** Dense harmonic sections, textural passages

### Lage Lund — Refined Clarity
- **Signature:** Clean articulation, precise note choice
- **Phrasing:** Deliberate, considered spacing
- **Dynamics:** Controlled, tasteful swells
- **Contour:** Classical balance, elegant arcs
- **Application:** Standards, traditional forms with modern touch

### Ant Law — Intervallic Innovation
- **Signature:** Wide intervals, unusual note groupings
- **Phrasing:** Rhythmically complex but flowing
- **Dynamics:** Expressive variance
- **Contour:** Angular with lyrical resolution
- **Application:** Modern compositions, complex harmony

### Grant Green — Soulful Directness
- **Signature:** Clean single-note lines, minimal vibrato
- **Phrasing:** Straightforward, blues-rooted
- **Dynamics:** Consistent, confident
- **Contour:** Horizontal, groove-locked
- **Application:** Blues, soul-jazz, groove tunes

### George Benson — Fluid Virtuosity
- **Signature:** Seamless bebop runs, scatting unison
- **Phrasing:** Continuous flow, breathing with the line
- **Dynamics:** Energetic, building intensity
- **Contour:** Ascending builds, release patterns
- **Application:** Uptempo bebop, showpiece passages

### Steve Khan — Harmonic Explorer
- **Signature:** Sophisticated chord-melody, Latin influences
- **Phrasing:** Rhythmically aware, clave-connected
- **Dynamics:** Percussive attacks, sustain contrast
- **Contour:** Harmonic shapes dictating melody
- **Application:** Latin-jazz, chord-melody arrangements

### Charlie Parker — The Source
- **Signature:** Lightning bebop vocabulary, blues integration
- **Phrasing:** Endless invention, surprise connections
- **Dynamics:** Variable intensity, accent-driven
- **Contour:** Virtuosic arcs, resolution targets
- **Application:** All bebop passages, vocabulary foundation

### Wayne Shorter — Harmonic Poet
- **Signature:** Non-functional progressions, melody-driven harmony
- **Phrasing:** Asymmetric, unexpected lengths
- **Dynamics:** Dynamic narrative, story-telling
- **Contour:** Enigmatic shapes, unresolved questions
- **Application:** Compositional themes, harmonic exploration

### Thelonious Monk — Rhythmic Architect
- **Signature:** Angular intervals, rhythmic displacement
- **Phrasing:** Space and stab, silence as sound
- **Dynamics:** Accent-driven, percussive
- **Contour:** Geometric, architectural
- **Application:** Quirky sections, rhythmic emphasis, motif development

### Sonny Stitt — Bebop Precision
- **Signature:** Clean bebop execution, Parker-adjacent
- **Phrasing:** Flowing eighth-note streams
- **Dynamics:** Consistent swing intensity
- **Contour:** Classic bebop arcs
- **Application:** Traditional bebop passages, vocabulary study

# 6. MOTIF LIBRARY (M1–M7)

## Core Album Motifs

| ID | Name | Intervals | Rhythm | Character | Application |
|:--:|:-----|:----------|:-------|:----------|:------------|
| **M1** | **The Question** | Ascending P4 → M2 → m3 | Dotted quarter + 8th | Inquiry, opening | Theme openings, A sections |
| **M2** | **Blues Cry** | m3 bend → P5 → descending m6 | Triplet + long | Emotional, soulful | Blues passages, climaxes |
| **M3** | **Angular Cell** | M7 leap → m2 step → P4 | Syncopated 16ths | Modern, edgy | Angular sections, tension |
| **M4** | **Bebop Seed** | 1-2-3-5 ascending + enclosure | Continuous 8ths | Classic, flowing | ii-V-I passages, runs |
| **M5** | **Floating Fourth** | P4 stack ascending/descending | Displaced quarters | Modal, open | Pedal point sections |
| **M6** | **The Resolution** | Descending M7 → ascending m3 | Ritardando feel | Conclusive, warm | Endings, cadences |
| **M7** | **Textural Ghost** | Wide intervals with rests | Sparse, rubato | Atmospheric | Intros, transitions |

## 6.1 Motif DNA Sequences

Each motif carries DNA that should be preserved through transformations:

```
M1: [+5, +2, -3]           // Interval sequence in semitones
M2: [+3↑bend, +7, -8]      // ↑bend indicates blue note treatment
M3: [+11, -1, +5]          // Wide leap followed by step
M4: [+2, +2, +4, +/-enc]   // Enclosure as final element
M5: [+5, +5, +5] or [-5, -5, -5]  // P4 stacks
M6: [-11, +3]              // Wide descent, small ascent
M7: [variable, REST, variable, REST]  // Space as structure
```

# 7. TRANSFORMATION RULES

## 7.1 Permissible Transformations

| Transform | Symbol | Description | Preservation |
|:----------|:------:|:------------|:-------------|
| **Transposition** | T | Move to new pitch center | All intervals preserved |
| **Inversion** | I | Mirror intervals around axis | Contour inverted, intervals preserved |
| **Retrograde** | R | Reverse note order | Rhythm often modified |
| **Augmentation** | A | Double rhythmic values | Intervals preserved |
| **Diminution** | D | Halve rhythmic values | Intervals preserved |
| **Fragmentation** | F | Use portion of motif | Core intervals must remain |
| **Sequence** | S | Repeat at new pitch level | Exact interval pattern |
| **Rhythmic Displacement** | RD | Shift across beat/bar | Pitches preserved |
| **Octave Displacement** | OD | Move notes to different octave | Interval quality preserved |
| **Harmonic Recoloring** | HR | Same melody, new harmony | Melodic contour preserved |

## 7.2 Transformation Chains

Complex developments combine transformations:

| Chain | Symbol | Example |
|:------|:------:|:--------|
| Transpose + Invert | T+I | M1 up P4, then inverted |
| Fragment + Sequence | F+S | First 3 notes of M2, sequenced |
| Augment + Displace | A+RD | M4 doubled, displaced by 8th |
| Retrograde + Octave | R+OD | M3 backwards, octave shifted |

## 7.3 Minimum Transformation Requirements

- **Every tune:** At least 2 motif transformations.
- **Theme returns:** Must show at least 1 transformation from original.
- **Development sections:** Minimum 3 different transformations.
- **Bridge sections:** Transformation or new motif required.

# 9. GLOBAL INVARIANTS

## 9.1 Mandatory Rules (Always Apply)

1. **Motif Presence:** Every 8-bar section must use at least one MAMS core motif (M1–M7).
2. **Transformation Requirement:** Every tune must contain at least two motif transformations.
3. **Interval Signature:** Every melody must express at least one MAMS interval signature (P4, M7, m6, #11, 2–6–♭3–#4).
4. **Blues DNA:** Must appear in lyrical or roots-influenced tunes.
5. **Bebop DNA:** Must appear in functional ii–V–I passages.
6. **Modern DNA:** Intervallic and polyrhythmic DNA must appear in modern, angular, or experimental sections.
7. **Expressive DNA:** Hall, Frisell, or Halvorson expressive character must shape phrasing and contour in at least one section per tune.
8. **Album Consistency:** Motif usage must be consistent across the album (M1 must sound recognizably like M1 in any tune).

## 9.2 Conditional Rules

| Condition | Rule |
|:----------|:-----|
| Tempo < 80 BPM | Prioritize Space (R-6), Expressive DNA from Hall/Frisell |
| Tempo > 160 BPM | Prioritize Bebop DNA, R-1 or R-2 |
| Blues Form | Minimum 60% Blues DNA |
| Shorter-style Harmony | Non-functional rules, M7 leaps, Shorter expressive DNA |
| Odd Meter | Emphasize additive rhythms (R-4), modern intervals |

# 11. MELODIC STYLE ENGINE INTEGRATION

## 11.1 Unified Generation Pipeline

```
INPUT: Chord progression (LeadSheet.md) + Style designation

STEP 1: Load MAMS.md (this document)
STEP 2: Load Melodic Style Engine (influence profiles)
STEP 3: Analyze chord progression for:
        - Functional passages (ii-V-I) → Bebop DNA
        - Static/modal passages → Modern DNA
        - Blues elements → Blues DNA

STEP 4: Select primary motifs (M1–M7) based on style
STEP 5: Apply interval DNA per section
STEP 6: Apply rhythmic DNA per section
STEP 7: Shape phrases using Phrase-Shape Archetypes

STEP 9: Execute planned transformations
STEP 10: Verify against Global Invariants
STEP 11: Run Consistency Checks

OUTPUT: Melody + Verification Report
```
